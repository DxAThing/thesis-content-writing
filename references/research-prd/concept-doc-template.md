# v1 Research Concept Document Template

```markdown
# 【Research Topic Name】(provisional)

## One-Line Position
> This research aims to solve [technical challenge] in [field],
> via [method direction],
> expected to achieve [contribution].

## Research Motivation
- **Current SOTA limitation**: [What cant current methods do well?]
- **Why this matters**: [Real-world failure case or practical gap]
- **Existing workarounds and their shortcomings**: [What has been tried and why it is not enough]

## Core Technical Challenges
1. **Challenge 1**: [Description + why it is hard]
2. **Challenge 2**: [Description + why it is hard]

## Expected Contribution Directions
(List directions only. Do not expand details — that is for v2.)
1. [Direction 1: e.g., a new representation for X]
2. [Direction 2: e.g., a new training strategy for Y]
3. [Optional: Direction 3]

## Scope Boundaries — What We Do NOT Do
- [Boundary 1: what is explicitly out of scope and why]
- [Boundary 2]

## Hypotheses to Validate
- **Hypothesis 1**: [Assumption] → Validation: [How to test before committing]
- **Hypothesis 2**: [Assumption] → Validation: [How to test]

## Open Questions
- [ ] [Question that must be answered to proceed]
- [ ] [Question that affects scope or technical route]
```

## Usage Notes

- Keep it concise: 1-2 pages maximum. The purpose is direction alignment, not detail.
- Every section should be answerable from the three-dimension diagnostic.
- "Scope Boundaries" is critical: it prevents scope creep and helps focus.
- "Hypotheses to Validate" forces explicit acknowledgment of assumptions.
